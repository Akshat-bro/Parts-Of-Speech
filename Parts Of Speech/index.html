Nouns

Nouns are words that name people, places, things, or ideas. They can be singular or plural.

Examples: cat, dog, city, happiness

Pronouns

Pronouns are words that replace nouns to avoid repetition. They can refer to people or things.

Examples: he, she, it, they

Verbs

Verbs are words that express actions or states of being. They are essential to the structure of a sentence.

Examples: run, jump, be, have

Adjectives

Adjectives are words that describe or modify nouns or pronouns, giving more information about them.

Examples: happy, sad, large, small

Adverbs

Adverbs are words that modify verbs, adjectives, or other adverbs. They often tell how, when, where, or to what extent.

Examples: quickly, very, well, tomorrow

Prepositions

Prepositions are words that show the relationship between a noun or pronoun and other words in a sentence.

Examples: in, on, at, by

Conjunctions

Conjunctions are words that connect words, phrases, or clauses in a sentence.

Examples: and, but, or, because

Interjections

Interjections are words or phrases that express strong emotions or sudden bursts of feeling.

Examples: oh, wow, ouch, hooray
